Tracks total payment count and summed amount over time to identify growth trends, seasonality, and revenue momentum across the business.

SQL

Payment Volume by Industry Vertical horizontal_bar 7 rows

Reveals which industry verticals generate the most payment activity, helping prioritize sales and support resources toward the highest-value segments.

  • Digital Services, Media & Telecoms dominates** with £1.19M in payment volume, accounting for ~29% of total activity — nearly 1.5x the second-highest vertical and the clear priority for sales investment.
  • Top 3 verticals (Digital Services, Professional/Financial Services, Sports & Fitness) contribute ~67%** of total payment volume (£4.28M combined), suggesting resources should be heavily concentrated here.
  • Healthcare and Societies & Clubs are underperforming** relative to their market potential, generating £368K and £171K respectively — representing growth opportunities with targeted outreach or product improvements.
SQL
SELECT o.parent_vertical AS `Industry Vertical`, SUM(p.amount) AS `Total Payment Amount`
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id
JOIN organisations o ON m.organisation_id = o.id
GROUP BY o.parent_vertical
ORDER BY `Total Payment Amount` DESC
LIMIT 30

SQL
SELECT o.id AS organisation_id, SUM(p.amount) AS total_revenue
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id
JOIN organisations o ON m.organisation_id = o.id
GROUP BY o.id
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC
LIMIT 15

SQL
SELECT
  o.id AS organisation_id,
  COUNT(p.id) AS payment_count,
  AVG(p.amount) AS avg_amount
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id
JOIN organisations o ON m.organisation_id = o.id
GROUP BY o.id
ORDER BY payment_count DESC
LIMIT 30
