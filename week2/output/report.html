Monthly Payment Volume & Revenue Trend (Mar 2018 – Mar 2020) line 13 rows

Tracks how total payment count and GBP revenue have grown over time, revealing seasonality, growth phases, and any revenue dips that need attention.

  • Explosive early growth:** Payment volume surged from just 1 transaction in Mar 2018 to 4,289 by Dec 2018, representing a 4,000x+ increase in 9 months, with revenue scaling proportionally from £0.50 to £5,494.
  • Revenue-per-payment compression:** In Apr 2018, average revenue per payment was £1.18; by Dec 2018 it dropped to £1.28 — but Aug 2018 shows a dip where revenue (£3,132) slightly *fell* despite volume rising from Jul's 2,302 to 2,431, signaling a pricing or mix issue.
  • Consistent Oct–Nov plateau:** Payments grew 6% (3,713→3,953) but revenue was virtually flat (£4,350→£4,346), suggesting volume growth stalled in monetization efficiency heading into Q4.
SQL
SELECT
    DATE_FORMAT(p.created_at, '%Y-%m') AS month,
    COUNT(*) AS total_payment_count,
    SUM(p.amount / 100) AS total_revenue_gbp
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id
JOIN organisations o ON m.organisation_id = o.id
WHERE p.currency = 'GBP'
  AND p.created_at >= '2018-03-01'
  AND p.created_at < '2020-04-01'
GROUP BY DATE_FORMAT(p.created_at, '%Y-%m')
ORDER BY month ASC
LIMIT 30

Revenue by Industry Vertical horizontal_bar 7 rows

Compares total GBP revenue contributed by each industry vertical to identify the highest-value customer segments and guide sales/marketing prioritisation.

  • Digital Services, Media & Telecoms** is the top-performing vertical at £9,582, contributing 23% of total revenue (£40,989), making it the clear priority for sales and marketing investment.
  • The top 3 verticals** (Digital Services, Professional & Financial Services, Sports & Fitness) collectively generate £24,439 — nearly 60% of total revenue — suggesting concentrated opportunity in these segments.
  • Societies & Clubs** is the lowest-value vertical at £1,714, generating 4.2% of total revenue and less than one-fifth of the top vertical's contribution, indicating it warrants reduced resource allocation or a targeted growth strategy.
SQL
SELECT
    o.parent_vertical AS `Industry Vertical`,
    SUM(p.amount / 100) AS `Total Revenue (£)`
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id
JOIN organisations o ON m.organisation_id = o.id
WHERE p.currency = 'GBP'
GROUP BY o.parent_vertical
ORDER BY `Total Revenue (£)` DESC
LIMIT 30

Payment Count by Industry Vertical bar 7 rows

Shows transaction volume per vertical so that high-volume but lower-value verticals (e.g. Sports & Fitness) can be distinguished from high-value, lower-volume ones (e.g. Digital Services).

  • Sports & Fitness dominates transaction volume** with 12,170 payments—50% more than the second-highest vertical (Tradesmen at 8,080) and 3x more than Professional & Financial Services (5,126).

SQL
SELECT o.parent_vertical, COUNT(p.id) AS number_of_payments
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id

Payment Source Mix: Volume vs Revenue Share bar 3 rows

Compares dashboard, app, and API channels across both payment count and total revenue to reveal which integration channel drives the most value and where to invest in tooling.

  • Dashboard dominates both metrics**: With 16,956 payments (53% of volume) generating £25,936 (63% of revenue), the dashboard channel delivers the highest revenue-per-payment at ~£1.53, making it the clear priority for tooling investment.
  • App shows volume-revenue mismatch**: Despite handling 13,045 payments (41% of volume), app generates only £10,459 (26% of revenue) at ~£0.80 per payment — less than half the dashboard rate — signaling lower-value transactions or pricing inefficiencies.
  • API is underpenetrated but efficient**: Only 1,792 payments (6% of volume) yet yields £3,594 (~£2.01 per payment), the highest revenue-per-transaction of all channels, suggesting strong ROI potential if API adoption is scaled.
SQL

Mandate Productivity by Scheme (Avg Payments per Mandate) bar 2 rows

Contrasts BACS and SEPA_CORE mandates on average payments generated per mandate, quantifying the 30% productivity premium of SEPA and informing scheme expansion decisions.

  • SEPA_CORE mandates generate an average of 5.36 payments per mandate, compared to 3.91 for BACS — a difference of 1.45 additional payments per mandate.
  • SEPA_CORE delivers a **37% productivity premium** over BACS (not 30%), meaning each SEPA mandate drives significantly more revenue utilization than its BACS counterpart.
  • For scheme expansion decisions, migrating or acquiring mandates under SEPA_CORE could yield approximately **1.45 more transactions per mandate**, compounding materially at scale — prioritizing SEPA growth over BACS could substantially improve collection efficiency.
SQL
SELECT
    m.scheme AS `Payment Scheme`,
    COUNT(p.id) / COUNT(DISTINCT m.id) AS `Avg Payments per Mandate`
FROM mandates m
JOIN payments p ON p.mandate_id = m.id
WHERE m.scheme IN ('BACS', 'SEPA_CORE')
GROUP BY m.scheme
ORDER BY `Avg Payments per Mandate` DESC
LIMIT 30

Average Payment Value by Industry Vertical horizontal_bar 7 rows

Reveals which verticals command the highest average transaction size, helping prioritise upsell efforts and identify verticals with premium pricing power.

  • Societies & Clubs dominates** with an average payment value of £7.52 — more than double the next highest vertical (Digital Services at £3.34), signalling strong upsell potential and premium pricing power worth prioritising.
  • A sharp mid-tier drop-off exists**: Healthcare (£2.79) and Property (£2.51) cluster closely together, while Professional & Financial Services falls to £1.56 — a 38% decline suggesting pricing pressure or lower-value transaction types in that segment.
  • Sports/Fitness and Tradesmen are low-value outliers** at £0.59 and £0.47 respectively — roughly 16x below Societies & Clubs — indicating either volume-driven models or untapped upsell opportunities requiring targeted intervention.
SQL
SELECT
    o.parent_vertical AS `Industry Vertical`,
    AVG(p.amount / 100) AS `Average Payment Value (£)`
FROM payments p
JOIN mandates m ON p.mandate_id = m.id
JOIN organisations o ON m.organisation_id = o.id
WHERE p.currency = 'GBP'
  AND o.parent_vertical IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY o.parent_vertical
ORDER BY `Average Payment Value (£)` DESC
LIMIT 30

New Mandate Creation vs New Organisation Onboarding Over Time line 13 rows

Overlays monthly new mandate authorisations against new organisation sign-ups to assess whether existing merchants are expanding their customer base or whether growth is purely driven by new merchant acquisition.

  • Mandate growth decoupled from org onboarding:** New mandates surged from 114 to 990 (+768%) between April and May 2018, while new organisations only grew from 132 to 153—confirming existing merchants rapidly expanded their customer base.
  • Org onboarding stopped entirely after June:** Zero new organisations were recorded from July 2018 onwards, yet mandates remained substantial (701–1,183/month), proving sustained growth was driven entirely by existing merchants acquiring new customers.
  • Peak mandate volume coincided with last active onboarding month:** June 2018 saw the highest mandates (1,183) alongside 136 new organisations, suggesting the final onboarding cohort immediately activated high-volume mandate collection upon joining.
SQL
SELECT
    month,
    SUM(new_mandates) AS new_mandates,
    SUM(new_organisations) AS new_organisations
FROM (
    SELECT
        DATE_FORMAT(created_at, '%Y-%m') AS month,
        COUNT(*) AS new_mandates,
        0 AS new_organisations
    FROM mandates
    GROUP BY DATE_FORMAT(created_at, '%Y-%m')
    UNION ALL
    SELECT
        DATE_FORMAT(created_at, '%Y-%m') AS month,
        0 AS new_mandates,
        COUNT(*) AS new_organisations
    FROM organisations
    GROUP BY DATE_FORMAT(created_at, '%Y-%m')
) combined
GROUP BY month
ORDER BY month
LIMIT 30
